<p><h3>6.00 Introduction to Computer Science and Programming
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Undergrad" title="Undergrad" src="/icns/under.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Fall" title="Fall" src="/icns/fall.gif">, <img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Spring" title="Spring" src="/icns/spring.gif">) <img src="/icns/rest.gif" alt="Rest Elec in Sci & Tech" title="Rest Elec in Sci & Tech" width="16" height="16" align="bottom">
<br>Prereq: None
<br>Units: 3-7-2
<br><a href="editcookie.cgi?add=6.00"><img align="bottom" border=1 width=16 height=16 alt="Add to schedule" title="Add to schedule" src="/icns/button0.gif"></a> <b>Lecture:</b> <i>MW3-4.30</i> (<a href="http://whereis.mit.edu/map-jpg?mapterms=26">26-100</a>) <b>Lab:</b> <i>TBA</i> <i><b>+final</b></i><!--s-->

Meets with 6.0001 first half of term and 6.0002 second half of term. Credit cannot also be received for 6.0001 or 6.0002. Final given during final exam week.

<p><h3>6.002 Circuits and Electronics
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Undergrad" title="Undergrad" src="/icns/under.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Fall" title="Fall" src="/icns/fall.gif">, <img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Spring" title="Spring" src="/icns/spring.gif">) <img src="/icns/rest.gif" alt="Rest Elec in Sci & Tech" title="Rest Elec in Sci & Tech" width="16" height="16" align="bottom">

Resistive elements and networks, independent and dependent sources, switches and MOS devices, digital abstraction, amplifiers, and energy storage elements. Dynamics of first- and second-order networks; design in the time and frequency domains; analog and digital circuits and applications. Design exercises. Occasional laboratory.

<a name="6.008"></a>
<p><h3>6.008 Introduction to Inference
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Undergrad" title="Undergrad" src="/icns/under.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Fall" title="Fall" src="/icns/fall.gif">) <img src="/icns/Lab.gif" alt="Institute Lab" title="Institute Lab" width="16" height="16" align="bottom">
<br>Prereq: <a href="./search.cgi?search=&cred=m2" title="18.02, 18.02A, 18.022, 18.023, 18.024">Calculus II (GIR)</a> or permission of instructor
<br>Units: 4-4-4
<br><a href="editcookie.cgi?add=6.008"><img align="bottom" border=1 width=16 height=16 alt="Add to schedule" title="Add to schedule" src="/icns/button0.gif"></a> <b>Lecture:</b> <i>MW10</i> (<a href="http://whereis.mit.edu/map-jpg?mapterms=32">32-155</a>) <b>Lab:</b> <i>TBA</i> <b>Recitation:</b> <i>TR10</i> (<a href="http://whereis.mit.edu/map-jpg?mapterms=34">34-303</a>) or <i>TR1</i> (<a href="http://whereis.mit.edu/map-jpg?mapterms=34">34-302</a>) or <i>TR2</i> (<a href="http://whereis.mit.edu/map-jpg?mapterms=34">34-302</a>) <i><b>+final</b></i><!--s-->
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif">
<br>Introduces probabilistic modeling for problems of inference and machine learning from data, emphasizing analytical and computational aspects. Distributions, marginalization, conditioning, and structure; graphical representations. Belief propagation, decision-making, classification, estimation, and prediction. Sampling methods and analysis. Introduces asymptotic analysis and information measures. Computational laboratory component explores the concepts introduced in class in the context contemporary applications. <p><h3>6.231 Dynamic Programming and Stochastic Control
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Graduate" title="Graduate" src="/icns/grad.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Spring" title="Spring" src="/icns/spring.gif">)
<br>Prereq: <a href="m6a.html#6.041B">6.041B</a> or <a href="m18b.html#18.600">18.600</a>; <a href="m18a.html#18.100A">18.100A</a>, <a href="m18a.html#18.100B">18.100B</a>, or <a href="m18a.html#18.100Q">18.100Q</a>
<br>Units: 4-0-8
<br>URL: <a href="http://web.mit.edu/6.231/www/6231.html">http://web.mit.edu/6.231/www/6231.html</a>
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif">
<br>Sequential decision-making via dynamic programming. Unified approach to optimal control of stochastic dynamic systems and Markovian decision problems. Applications in linear-quadratic control, inventory control, resource allocation, scheduling, and planning. Optimal decision making under perfect and imperfect state information. Certainty equivalent, open loop-feedback control, rollout, model predictive control, aggregation, and other suboptimal control methods. Infinite horizon problems: discounted, stochastic shortest path, average cost, and semi-Markov models. Value and policy iteration. Abstract models in dynamic programming. Approximate/neurodynamic programming. Simulation based methods. Discussion of current research on the solution of large-scale problems.
<br><I>J. N. Tsitsiklis</I><br>
</p><!--end-->

<p><h3>6.252[J] Nonlinear Optimization
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Graduate" title="Graduate" src="/icns/grad.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Spring" title="Spring" src="/icns/spring.gif">)

<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif">

<p><h3>6.256 Algebraic Techniques and Semidefinite Optimization
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Not offered academic year 2018-2019" title="Not offered academic year 2018-2019" src="/icns/nonext.gif"><img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Graduate" title="Graduate" src="/icns/grad.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Spring" title="Spring" src="/icns/spring.gif">)
<br>Prereq: <a href="m6a.html#6.251">6.251</a> or <a href="m6a.html#6.255">6.255</a>
<br>Units: 3-0-9
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif">
<br>Theory and computational techniques for optimization problems involving polynomial equations and inequalities with particular, emphasis on the connections with semidefinite optimization. Develops algebraic and numerical approaches of general applicability, with a view towards methods that simultaneously incorporate both elements, stressing convexity-based ideas, complexity results, and efficient implementations. Examples from several engineering areas, in particular systems and control applications. Topics include semidefinite programming, resultants/discriminants, hyperbolic polynomials, Groebner bases, quantifier elimination, and sum of squares.
<br><I>P. Parrilo</I><br>
</p><!--end-->

<p><h3>6.262 Discrete Stochastic Processes
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Graduate" title="Graduate" src="/icns/grad.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Spring" title="Spring" src="/icns/spring.gif">)
<br>Prereq: <a href="m6a.html#6.041B">6.041B</a>, <a href="m6b.html#6.431B">6.431B</a> or <a href="m18a.html#18.204">18.204</a>
<br>Units: 4-0-8
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif">
<br>Review of probability and laws of large numbers; Poisson counting process and renewal processes; Markov chains (including Markov decision theory), branching processes, birth-death processes, and semi-Markov processes; continuous-time Markov chains and reversibility; random walks, martingales, and large deviations; applications from queueing, communication, control, and operations research.
<br><I>R. G. Gallager, V. W. S. Chan</I><br>

<p><h3>6.263[J] Data-Communication Networks
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Not offered academic year 2017-2018" title="Not offered academic year 2017-2018" src="/icns/nooffer.gif"><img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Graduate" title="Graduate" src="/icns/grad.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Fall" title="Fall" src="/icns/fall.gif">)

<br>Units: 3-0-9

Draws upon concepts from queueing theory and optimization.

<p><h3>6.265[J] Advanced Stochastic Processes
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Graduate" title="Graduate" src="/icns/grad.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Spring" title="Spring" src="/icns/spring.gif">)
<br>(Same subject as <A HREF="m15a.html#15.070">15.070[J]</A>)
<br>Prereq: <a href="m6b.html#6.431B">6.431B</a>, <a href="m15a.html#15.085">15.085J</a>, <a href="m18a.html#18.100A">18.100A</a>, <a href="m18a.html#18.100B">18.100B</a>, or <a href="m18a.html#18.100Q">18.100Q</a>

<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif">

<br><I>D. Gamarnik, G. Bresler</I><br>

<p><h3>6.267 Heterogeneous Networks: Architecture, Transport, Proctocols, and Management
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Not offered academic year 2018-2019" title="Not offered academic year 2018-2019" src="/icns/nonext.gif"><img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Graduate" title="Graduate" src="/icns/grad.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Fall" title="Fall" src="/icns/fall.gif">)
<br>Prereq: <a href="m6a.html#6.041B">6.041B</a> or <a href="m6a.html#6.042">6.042</a>
<br>Units: 4-0-8
<br><a href="editcookie.cgi?add=6.267"><img align="bottom" border=1 width=16 height=16 alt="Add to schedule" title="Add to schedule" src="/icns/button0.gif"></a> <b>Lecture:</b> <i>MW9.30-11</i> (<a href="http://whereis.mit.edu/map-jpg?mapterms=34">34-303</a>) <b>Recitation:</b> <i>F9.30-11</i> (<a href="http://whereis.mit.edu/map-jpg?mapterms=34">34-303</a>)<!--s-->
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif">
<br>Introduction to modern heterogeneous networks and the provision of heterogeneous services. Architectural principles, analysis, algorithmic techniques, performance analysis, and existing designs are developed and applied to understand current problems in network design and architecture. Begins with basic principles of networking. Emphasizes development of mathematical and algorithmic tools; applies them to understanding network layer design from the performance and scalability viewpoint. Concludes with network management and control, including the architecture and performance analysis of interconnected heterogeneous networks. Provides background and insight to understand current network literature and to perform research on networks with the aid of network design projects.
<br><I>V. W. S. Chan, R. G. Gallager</I><br>No textbook information available

<p><h3>6.268 Network Science and Models
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif"></h3>
<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Graduate" title="Graduate" src="/icns/grad.gif">  (<img width=16 height=16 align="bottom" alt="Spring" title="Spring" src="/icns/spring.gif">)
<br>Prereq: <a href="m6a.html#6.041B">6.041B</a>, <a href="m18a.html#18.06">18.06</a>
<br>Units: 3-0-9
<br><img alt="______" src="/icns/hr.gif">
<br>Introduces the main mathematical models used to describe large networks and dynamical processes that evolve on networks. Static models of random graphs, preferential attachment, and other graph evolution models.  Epidemic propagation, opinion dynamics, social learning, and inference in networks. Applications drawn from social, economic, natural, and infrastructure networks, as well as networked decision systems such as sensor networks.
<br><I>P. Jaillet, J. N. Tsitsiklis</I><br>
